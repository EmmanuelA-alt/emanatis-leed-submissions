4.1 Purpose

The mock-up panel verifies that the specified materials, mix ratios, application technique, and curing protocol produce plaster meeting all quality acceptance criteria (§5.2) before production work begins. It also serves as the physical benchmark against which all production work is visually compared.

4.2 Mock-Up Panel Requirements

RequirementSpecification
SizeMinimum 2m × 2m on each substrate type present: (a) concrete wall, (b) masonry/brick wall, (c) concrete beam infill (δοκαράκι). If only one substrate type exists in quantity, one 2m × 2m panel is acceptable with Chairman approval.
LocationBasement wall representative of in-situ conditions. Approved mock-up remains on site throughout plastering as the acceptance benchmark.
ApplicationAll three coats (scratch + brown + finish), full thickness (26mm), using the EXACT materials, mix ratios, mixing procedure, tools, and curing protocol specified in this document.
Cure period28 days minimum. Full curing protocol (§3.7) must be followed — mist cure 2-3× daily for first 3 days, no accelerated drying.
TimingMock-up must be applied and cured BEFORE any production plastering begins. Allow 28+ days from mock-up application to production start.

7.1 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

ItemRequirementWhen

7.2 Lime Hazard

WARNING: Lime putty is CAUSTIC — pH ~12.4. Causes serious eye damage and skin burns. All personnel handling lime must be trained on SDS content. Immediate first aid: flush eyes with running water for 15 minutes minimum — seek medical attention. Skin contact: remove contaminated clothing, wash with water and soap.

Eye wash station + running water must be accessible within 30 seconds of the mixing area. First aid kit on site. SDS binder in site office with Safety Data Sheets for ALL materials (cement, lime, sand, marble dust, mesh, beads) per REACH/CLP regulations.

7.3 Mixing Area

Must be well-ventilated. No confined-space mixing of dry cement/lime without FFP2 mask and mechanical ventilation. Mixing area to be cordoned off from other trades during dry mixing operations. Clean water source within 10m.

7.4 HVAC Protection

EQc3.1 REQUIREMENT: Seal ALL duct openings, return grilles, and ventilation shafts with polyethylene sheeting and tape BEFORE any wet plastering begins. Photograph before sealing + after sealing. Remove seals only after plaster is fully cured and dust has settled (minimum 28 days). These photos are mandatory for the IAQ documentation package.

8.3 EQc2 — Inherently Non-Emitting Strategy

Compliance path: Cement-lime plaster composed exclusively of mineral/inorganic materials (cement, lime, sand, marble dust, water). NO polymer additives, NO organic fibers. Qualifies as inherently non-emitting IF supported by chemical ingredient inventory from cement and lime suppliers confirming no organic additives at ≥0.1% (1,000 ppm). This avoids VOC chamber testing entirely.

Critical document: Chemical ingredient inventory from Lafarge Hellas and lime putty supplier. Without this, the EQc2 claim fails.
